About Sweden

Sweden, officially the Kingdom of Sweden, is a Nordic country located in Northern Europe. With its stunning landscapes, innovative culture, and high quality of life, Sweden consistently ranks among the world's most livable countries.

From the picturesque archipelagos of Stockholm to the vast wilderness of Lapland, Sweden offers visitors a perfect blend of urban sophistication and natural beauty, all infused with the famous Swedish concept of "lagom" (just the right amount).

  • Capital: Stockholm
  • Official Language: Swedish
  • Population: 10+ million
  • Currency: Swedish Krona (SEK)
  • Government: Constitutional Monarchy

History

Sweden's history spans thousands of years, from the Viking Age (8th-11th centuries) when Norse seafarers explored, traded, and raided across Europe, to its emergence as a major European power in the 17th century under King Gustav II Adolf.

In modern times, Sweden has transformed from an agricultural society into one of the world's most innovative economies. Since 1814, Sweden has maintained a policy of neutrality in armed conflicts, which kept it out of both World Wars. Today, Sweden is known for its progressive social policies, technological innovation, and commitment to sustainability.

Culture

Swedish culture is characterized by equality, modesty, and a deep appreciation for nature. The concept of "allemansrätten" (the right of public access) allows everyone to roam freely in nature, reflecting Swedes' close relationship with their natural environment.

Sweden has given the world iconic cultural exports, from ABBA and Swedish design to literary figures like Astrid Lindgren (creator of Pippi Longstocking) and Stieg Larsson. Swedish traditions like Midsummer celebrations, Lucia Day, and fika (coffee break) are cherished aspects of daily life that visitors can experience.

Famous Landmarks

Stockholm City Hall

One of Sweden's most famous buildings, the City Hall hosts the Nobel Prize banquet annually. Its iconic tower offers panoramic views of Stockholm's beautiful archipelago.

Ice Hotel

Located in Jukkasjärvi, this unique hotel is rebuilt each winter entirely from ice and snow. It features stunning ice sculptures and offers guests a truly unforgettable Arctic experience.

Gamla Stan (Old Town)

Stockholm's charming old town dates back to the 13th century. With its narrow cobblestone streets, colorful buildings, and medieval architecture, it's one of Europe's best-preserved historic centers.
