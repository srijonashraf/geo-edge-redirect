About the United States

The United States of America is a diverse and vast country spanning 3.8 million square miles across North America. With 50 states, it's home to over 330 million people from countless cultural backgrounds, making it one of the most multicultural nations in the world.

From the bustling streets of New York City to the serene landscapes of the Grand Canyon, the United States offers an incredible variety of experiences, landscapes, and cultures.

  • Capital: Washington, D.C.
  • Largest City: New York City
  • Official Language: No official language at federal level (English is predominant)
  • Population: 330+ million
  • Currency: United States Dollar (USD)

History

The United States declared independence from Great Britain on July 4, 1776, an event celebrated annually as Independence Day. The American Revolution followed, establishing the world's first constitutional democracy based on the principles of liberty, equality, and self-governance.

Throughout its history, the United States has experienced significant transformations—from the Civil War and the abolition of slavery to the Civil Rights Movement, becoming a global superpower after World War II, and continuing to evolve as a diverse, dynamic society in the 21st century.

Culture

American culture is a rich tapestry woven from diverse influences. Often described as a "melting pot" or "salad bowl," the United States embraces contributions from indigenous peoples, European settlers, African Americans, Latin Americans, Asians, and immigrants from around the world.

From Hollywood movies and Broadway shows to jazz, rock 'n' roll, hip-hop, and country music, American cultural exports have influenced global entertainment. American literature, art, and cuisine similarly reflect the nation's diverse heritage and innovative spirit.

Famous Landmarks

Statue of Liberty

A symbol of freedom and democracy, the Statue of Liberty stands on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, welcoming visitors and immigrants to the United States.

Grand Canyon

One of the world's most spectacular natural wonders, the Grand Canyon in Arizona showcases millions of years of geological history through its colorful layers.

The White House

Located in Washington, D.C., the White House has been the official residence and workplace of the President of the United States since 1800.
