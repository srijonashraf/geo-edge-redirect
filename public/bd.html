Welcome to Bangladesh

The Land of Rivers and Rich Cultural Heritage

About Bangladesh

Bangladesh is a beautiful South Asian country known for its lush landscapes, vibrant culture, and warm hospitality. Located at the apex of the Bay of Bengal, it's a land of rivers, with the mighty Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna creating one of the world's largest deltas.

With a rich cultural heritage and a history dating back thousands of years, Bangladesh offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, historical sites, and cultural experiences.

  • Capital: Dhaka
  • Official Language: Bengali (Bangla)
  • Population: 165+ million
  • Currency: Bangladeshi Taka (BDT)
  • National Flower: Water Lily (Shapla)

History

Bangladesh's history is rich and complex, with evidence of civilization dating back over 4,000 years. The region was part of various empires and kingdoms throughout history, including the Maurya, Gupta, Pala, and Mughal empires.

After being part of British India and later East Pakistan, Bangladesh gained independence in 1971 following the Liberation War. Since then, the country has made remarkable progress in various sectors, including education, healthcare, and economic development.

Culture

Bangladesh has a rich cultural heritage influenced by Bengali, Islamic, and indigenous traditions. The country is known for its literature, music, dance, and art forms. Bengali literature has a history spanning over a thousand years, with Nobel laureate Rabindranath Tagore being one of its most celebrated figures.

Traditional Bengali music, including Baul, Lalon, and Rabindra Sangeet, forms an integral part of the cultural identity. The country is also famous for its colorful festivals, such as Pohela Boishakh (Bengali New Year), Eid, and Durga Puja, which showcase the harmonious coexistence of different religious communities.

Famous Landmarks

Ahsan Manzil (Pink Palace)

Located in Dhaka, this magnificent pink palace was the official residence of the Nawabs of Dhaka. Now a museum, it showcases the history and lifestyle of the Nawabs.

Sundarbans

The largest mangrove forest in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Sundarbans is home to the Royal Bengal Tiger and numerous other species of flora and fauna.

Cox's Bazar

Boasting the world's longest natural sea beach (120 km), Cox's Bazar is a popular tourist destination known for its breathtaking views, golden sands, and blue waters.
